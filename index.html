Hi, I'm Nick.

I’m a data scientist working to help the global aid community use data to make the world a better place.

About Me

Complex problems, like those in international development, produce complex data, and distilling it all down into meaningful and actionable insight takes time, talent, and tenacity. Many non-profits around the world are doing excellent work, but don’t have the resources to analyze, summarize, or even track much at all in the way of data. That’s where I come in. I love creating new tools and techniques for these organizations to maximize their impact in a robust data-driven way. As an engineer by training, I’ve been in the trenches of analytical tech for several years, during which I’ve helped major firms around the world, like HP and Blackberry, Boeing, and Raytheon, improve the reliability of their products with better data collection and analysis tools. But, what really excites me these days is working with organizations you probably haven’t heard as much about, like APOPO: a Dutch NGO that uses giant trained rats to clear minefields in post-conflict regions. I’ve got a knack for learning new tools to tackle new problems, which comes in handy when making strategy decisions about how best to support 3000 NGOs in over 160 countries. Outside the office, I’m a proud folk music street performer and fan of obscure musical techniques (Tuvan Throat Singing anyone?). If I’m not performing, I’m probably outside figuring out how to cram another planter into my tiny urban vegetable garden. I may not be able to grow all my produce on my apartment balcony, but that doesn’t mean I won’t keep trying.

Work

GlobalGiving

Senior Business Intelligence Analyst• April 2015 - Present

Business Intelligence Analyst• April 2013 - April 2015

Proinspire Fellow - Operations • May 2012 - April 2013

GlobalGiving is an online market for ideas, information, and money that democratizes aid and philanthropy in support of several thousand grassroots non-profits around the world. Since joining the organization as a ProInspire Fellow, I focus on using our data to answer critical strategy questions, building new data-driven tools to help make our data more digestible and accessible, and inventing new techniques for tracking our social impact. Some of the accomplishments I'm most proud of include designing and implementing an enterprise-level self-serve reporting solution to share insights from our databases with everyone on staff, creating an algorithm that has stopped over $250,000 in fraud by premptively detecting when illegitimate donations are made on the site, and fostering a culture of experimentation by advising colleagues on experimental design and statistical analysis.

Parametric Technology Corporation (PTC)

Reliability Consulting Engineer • November 2010 - May 2012

My work at PTC centered around configuring tools and systems for engineering firms large and small to better collect, measure, and analyze data around the reliability of their products. These implementations varied as widely as our customers, from a way to track armored vehicle maintenance and inventory across three continents simultaneously to a system for managing the reliability at each step of the supply chain of one of the largest celluar device manufacturers in the world to a process and tool to record and understand 100,000+ points of field failure data for anti-aircraft defense units. In addition, I designed and delivered several custom training courses on a wide range of reliability topics and redesigned our internal business process for developing statements of work to improve scoping accuracy, increase customer satisfaction, and manage $50K-$100K services jobs.
