About Me

I’m an experienced data wonk here to help social good organizations across the globe maximize their impact. Nonprofits and social entrepreneurs do great work every day, but many don’t have the resources, tools, or experience to make sense of their data. That’s where I come in. Finding creative ways to make development data accessible, understandable, and actionable for everyone is what gets me out of bed in the morning

I’ve got a knack for learning new tools to tackle new problems. Naturally, this comes in handy when making strategic decisions about how best to support 3000+ NGOs in 160+ countries, combatting fraud on a website that processes millions worth of transactions per year, or hacking together new ways of extracting meaning out of the aid sector’s famously complex and messy data.

When I’m not writing code, crunching numbers, or telling stories with data, I'm a proud folk music street performer, amateur haiku poet, and worm farmer.

I was invited by the Initiative for Open Ag Funding to join a four-day hackathon in London focused on using data science to make the public agriculture data more accessible and usable. I led a pilot effort to use natural language techniques to turn unstructured project documents into meanginful location data. Read my guest post on the Interaction blog about our work.

AidSight

As a capstone project for UC Berkeley's Master of Information and Data Science program, my team and I created a first-of-its-kind platform that identifies and fixes data quality gaps in all International Aid Transparency Initiative (IATI) datasets (10K organizations, 600K projects) using machine learning and data visualization implemented in MongoDB, Scikit-Learn, and D3. AidSight is now in real-world use at Bond, DFID, and other major aid agencies. Read more about AidSight at the Keystone Accountability blog or test it yourself at aidsight.org.

Close

Root Change Local Systems Analysis Platform

Root Change is a leader in capacity development for global nonprofits and, with funding from USAID's new Localworks program, they've enlisted me to reimagine their signature product, the Local Systems Analysis tool, for better scalability, feedback, and interoperability. The result is a new web platform (built on Flask, D3.js, MongoDB, and NetworkX) that enables large-scale network analysis of international development communities.
